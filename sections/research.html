Featured Finding

Norms and Networks: Women's Political Participation in Rural Peshawar

Our 2019 Peshawar study represents one of the most comprehensive door-to-door census surveys conducted on women's political participation in rural Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. By interviewing 3,738 households and mapping social network ties, we identified a critical pattern of pluralistic ignorance.

The evidence shows that while over 84% of women personally support their right to vote, they significantly underestimate support within their own communities — a perception gap reinforced by deeply parallel, gender-segregated social networks.
