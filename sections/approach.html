Operational Protocol

How operations are administered.

DFCA-Research administers each engagement under a fixed operational protocol. The establishment does not set research questions, define treatments, or author outputs. It receives a protocol from the commissioning principal investigator and executes that protocol in the field with structural discipline and strict administrative honesty.

A roster of more than two thousand trained field enumerators supports deployment across the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa and Punjab districts on record. Every deployment is subject to supervisor verification, backcheck procedures, and geo-tagged completion evidence for each captured unit.

01

Protocol Intake and Formalisation

The commissioning researcher's protocol, sampling frame, and instruments are received in writing. Scope, cost schedule, and administrative responsibilities are documented in a formal operational agreement.

02

Randomized Controlled Trial Administration

Personnel specifically trained to conduct rigorous RCT surveys are deployed according to the investigator's assignment scheme. Randomisation integrity and blinding conditions are preserved under documented supervisor oversight.

03

Treatment Administration

Field treatments are delivered exactly as specified by the investigator. The precise methodology of delivery — timing, wording, recipient confirmation — is recorded and reported back to the researcher without reinterpretation.

04

Field Monitoring and Evaluation Infrastructure

Monitoring dashboards and reporting pipelines are configured to allow the commissioning researcher to observe field progress actively, identify deviations early, and request protocol-consistent corrective action while the operation is live.

05

Verifiable Reporting with Geo-Tagged Evidence

Every completed unit of fieldwork is documented with geo-tagged coordinates, timestamp, enumerator identifier, and supervisor sign-off. The complete operational record is delivered to the researcher to ensure full transparency of execution.
