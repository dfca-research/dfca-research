Electricity Subsidy Experiment, Charsadda District

Charsadda District, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan  ·  December 2022 to 2024

DFCA Research conducted a three-wave household survey experiment across 1,698 households in Charsadda District, administering structured interviews at baseline (December 2022), midline (September 2023), and endline (2024). Households were randomly assigned through a lottery to one of four electricity subsidy treatment tiers or a no-intervention control group. DFCA mobilized monthly subsidy payments directly to treatment households via mobile money platforms, disbursing a total of PKR 792,730 across 1,097 enrolled households during the intervention period. The study tracked electricity billing amounts, consumption patterns, payment compliance, and observed illicit connection behavior across all three tehsils of Charsadda District.

Study Type Randomized controlled trial, three survey waves
Geography Charsadda District, KP, Pakistan (PESCO service area)
Field Period Dec 2022 (baseline) to 2024 (endline)
Sample 1,698 households at baseline; 1,589 at midline; 1,569 at endline
Treatment Arms Four subsidy tiers (T1 to T4) plus no-intervention control
Tehsils Covered Charsadda, Tangi, Shabqadar
Payment Mode JazzCash and EasyPaisa mobile accounts

Evidence from the Field

Five analytical views of the trial evidence.

Treatment effect estimates on monthly bill amounts (difference-in-differences)

All four treatment arms show a positive difference-in-differences estimate

Each dot represents one treatment arm's net change in billed amounts relative to the control group trend. All four estimates sit to the right of zero, indicating treated households collectively maintained or increased bills while the control group declined.

T1 records the largest DiD estimate: PKR +1,969 per month

T1 households received the lowest subsidy tier (mean PKR 409/month) yet show the strongest billing retention effect. Even modest monthly cash transfers appear sufficient to anchor consumption above the control trajectory.

T3 shows the smallest positive estimate: PKR +1,343 per month

Despite receiving the second-highest subsidy tier, T3 produced the smallest DiD. This breaks any monotonic dose-response assumption — higher subsidy amounts did not uniformly translate into larger billing effects.

All confidence intervals span zero — consistent direction, inconclusive precision

Horizontal error bars extend well past zero in both directions for every arm. Without accounting for feeder-level clustering, these intervals are illustrative bounds only. The directional pattern is consistent but not statistically conclusive.

Mean monthly bill trajectory across baseline and endline survey waves

At baseline, all five arms clustered between PKR 10,500 and PKR 11,500

The narrow baseline spread across all five arms — including the control — is the visual signature of successful randomization. Groups were statistically comparable before the intervention began in January 2023.

The control group's mean bill fell by PKR 481 between baseline and endline

Without any subsidy, the average household bill declined. This suggests electricity consumption was affordability-constrained — households reduced usage when no financial relief was provided over the study period.

Every treatment arm trended upward while the control declined

The diverging trajectories are the study's central finding. All four subsidized groups increased their mean billed amount from baseline, widening the gap against the falling control group mean over two years.

T2 reaches the highest mean endline bill at PKR 12,486 — a net gain of PKR 1,012

T2 households received a mean PKR 443/month subsidy and ended with the highest average bill in the study. Even modest monthly transfers sustained consumption well above pre-trial levels and above the declining control.

The control group shows a modest decline over the period, while all four treatment arms exhibit an upward trajectory by endline. Kernel density estimate of monthly electricity bill amounts by group and wave

Three smoothed distributions show the full shape of bill data across groups and waves

The curves represent estimated probability densities — where a distribution peaks is where most households' bills fall. All three curves are left-skewed, with peaks below PKR 5,000 and long right tails, reflecting the range of consumption in Charsadda.

At baseline, control and treated distributions are nearly identical

The close overlap of the navy and solid gold curves at baseline confirms that the randomization produced comparable groups. Both distributions share the same modal billing range before the intervention started.

By endline, the treated distribution shifts rightward toward higher bills

Compare the dashed gold curve (treated endline) to the solid gold (treated baseline). The peak shifts right and the tail extends further — treated households moved into higher billing bins over the study period.

The rightward shift is gradual — subsidies sustained consumption, not just spending

The treated endline distribution does not spike sharply — it broadens and shifts. This gradual migration toward higher billing bins suggests the subsidy enabled continued electricity use rather than driving one-time consumption increases.

Enumerator-observed illicit connection rates by arm and survey wave

↓2.9pp    ↑2.3pp    ↓0.8pp    ↑8.9pp    ↓4.9pp

At baseline, observed illicit connection rates ranged from 15% to 22% across arms

Enumerators recorded whether they observed a hooked (illicit) connection at each interview. Baseline rates varied across arms, setting the reference point for measuring change over the study period.

The control group declined modestly from 20.1% to 17.2% at endline

The untreated group showed a small decrease in observed theft over the study period. This baseline decline may reflect broader district-level changes independent of the subsidy intervention, and sets the counterfactual benchmark.

T4 (highest subsidy) shows the largest reduction: −4.9 percentage points

The arm receiving the most generous financial relief (mean PKR 1,253/month) showed the strongest observed reduction in illicit connections. A DiD of −2.0pp vs. control is suggestive, but the confidence interval spans zero.

T3 increased by +8.9 percentage points — results are heterogeneous and inconclusive

No arm produces a statistically reliable effect on theft behavior. The range of outcomes across arms, from −4.9pp to +8.9pp, reflects the noise in enumerator-observed measures and the small per-arm sample sizes.

Subsidy disbursement scale and payment confirmation at midline

DFCA disbursed PKR 792,730 across 1,097 households in a graduated four-tier structure

The bars show mean monthly subsidy per household, rising from T1 to T4. The gold line shows what fraction of households in each arm confirmed receiving their January 2023 payment at the midline interview in September 2023.

T4 received three times more per month than T1: PKR 1,253 vs. PKR 409

The graduated structure was designed to test dose-response effects. Monthly subsidies increased sharply from T3 (PKR 725) to T4 (PKR 1,253), with a smaller step between T1 and T2. Whether this dose gradient drives bill effects is a central research question.

Payment confirmation rates held above 93% across all four arms

The near-uniform confirmation line — flat across all four tiers — demonstrates operational reliability. DFCA's mobile money disbursement system performed consistently regardless of subsidy amount, with no arm falling below 93.8%.

T4 achieved the highest confirmation rate at 97.5% — large transfers were reliably delivered

The highest-value arm had the highest payment verification rate. This rules out operational failure as an explanation for any null effects in the highest-tier arm — the money reached households exactly as intended.

The gold line and right axis show the percentage of treatment households in each arm who confirmed receipt of their January 2023 payment at the midline interview. Confirmation rates ranged from 93.8 percent (T2) to 97.5 percent (T4). Field observations

What we observed.

Treatment households maintained higher billed consumption relative to the control group trend by endline.

The control group mean monthly bill declined by PKR 481 between baseline and endline. All four treatment arms showed net bill increases over the same period, producing DiD estimates ranging from PKR 1,343 (T3) to PKR 1,969 (T1). These estimates carry wide confidence intervals that do not account for clustering at the feeder level; they indicate a directional pattern rather than a statistically precise treatment effect.

Source: Electricity billing module (M2_1), baseline and endline. Charsadda District, 2022 and 2024.

DFCA disbursed PKR 792,730 across 1,097 treatment households, verified at a 95.2 percent confirmation rate.

Monthly subsidy amounts averaged PKR 409 per household in T1, PKR 443 in T2, PKR 725 in T3, and PKR 1,253 in T4, reflecting the graduated tier structure. At the midline survey, enumerators cross-checked each payment against the household's official electricity bill and collected photographic receipts from 1,060 households. Payment confirmation rates across all arms ranged from 93.8 to 97.5 percent.

Source: Midline payment module (xx_feb_pay, payment variable). September 2023.

Observed illicit connections showed a heterogeneous pattern across treatment arms, with no arm exhibiting a clear directional effect.

At baseline, hooked connection rates ranged from 15.0 percent (T3) to 21.9 percent (T4) across arms. By endline, T4 showed the largest net reduction (-4.9 percentage points) while T3 increased (+8.9 percentage points). The DiD for T4 relative to control was -2.0 percentage points, and for T3 was +11.8 percentage points. All DiD estimates for theft carry confidence intervals that span zero, making directional inference inconclusive from these survey-based observation data.

Source: P1 enumerator observation, baseline and endline. Charsadda District, 2022 and 2024.

Baseline household profiles were similar across arms, supporting the validity of the randomization.

At baseline, mean household head age was 43.2 years and 99.5 percent of household heads were male. Mean household size was 6.7 persons. Over half of household heads (51.6 percent) reported no formal education, and 21.9 percent held intermediate or higher qualifications. Mean monthly bill amounts at baseline ranged from PKR 10,523 (T1) to PKR 11,545 (Control), with substantial within-arm variance consistent with a heterogeneous sample across three tehsils.

Source: Baseline household roster and electricity billing module. Charsadda District, December 2022.

DFCA mobilized electricity subsidy payments to users in Charsadda District over a six-month intervention window beginning January 2023. Eligible households were enrolled through a baseline census conducted in December 2022 across three tehsils: Charsadda, Tangi, and Shabqadar. Randomization allocated 1,358 households across four subsidy treatment tiers, with 340 households assigned to a no-intervention control group. DFCA field teams coordinated payment delivery via mobile money platforms, collecting mobile account details at baseline and verifying receipt at midline through structured interview questions and photographic evidence. Managing this financial intervention required establishing substantial community trust across a geographically dispersed sample in rural Charsadda. DFCA executed the disbursements with strict operational integrity: enumerators cross-checked each payment against official PESCO bill reference numbers, documented receipt photographs for 1,060 of the 1,097 enrolled households, and flagged discrepancies for follow-up during the midline survey round. The midline survey conducted in September 2023 confirmed that 95.2 percent of payment recipients had received their January 2023 subsidy at the correct calculated amount.

"Across 1,097 enrolled treatment households, DFCA field teams confirmed receipt of PKR 792,730 in electricity subsidies with a 95.2 percent verification rate, supported by photographic bill receipts collected at midline."
