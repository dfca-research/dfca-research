Electricity Subsidy Experiment, Charsadda District

Charsadda District, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan  ·  December 2022 to 2024

DFCA Research conducted a three-wave household survey experiment across 1,698 households in Charsadda District, administering structured interviews at baseline (December 2022), midline (September 2023), and endline (2024). Households were randomly assigned through a lottery to one of four electricity subsidy treatment tiers or a no-intervention control group. DFCA mobilized monthly subsidy payments directly to treatment households via mobile money platforms, disbursing a total of PKR 805,570 across 1,116 enrolled households during the intervention period. The study tracked electricity billing amounts, consumption patterns, payment compliance, and observed illicit connection behavior across all three tehsils of Charsadda District.

Study Type Randomized controlled trial, three survey waves
Geography Charsadda District, KP, Pakistan (PESCO service area)
Field Period Dec 2022 (baseline) to 2024 (endline)
Sample 1,698 households at baseline; 1,589 at midline; 1,569 at endline
Treatment Arms Four subsidy tiers (T1 to T4) plus no-intervention control
Tehsils Covered Charsadda, Tangi, Shabqadar
Payment Mode JazzCash and EasyPaisa mobile accounts

Evidence from the Field

Five analytical views of the trial evidence.

Treatment effect estimates on monthly bill amounts (difference-in-differences)

Horizontal dots show difference-in-differences (DiD) estimates for mean monthly electricity bill amounts (PKR) by treatment arm, relative to the control group, comparing baseline (December 2022) to endline (2024). Estimates exclude four observations with monthly bills exceeding PKR 100,000 (three in the control group across both waves, one in T2 at endline) as implausible for residential meters. Horizontal lines represent approximate 95 percent confidence intervals; whiskers do not account for within-feeder clustering and should be treated as illustrative bounds. All four treatment arms show positive DiD estimates. All confidence intervals span zero.

Vertical reference line at zero. DiD = (Endline mean - Baseline mean) for arm minus the equivalent for Control. Four observations with M2_1 > PKR 100,000 excluded. n: baseline 330 (Control), 332 (T1), 321 (T2), 333 (T3), 326 (T4); endline 309 (Control), 307 (T1), 295 (T2), 308 (T3), 303 (T4). Approximate 95% CI: DiD ± 1.96 × SE.

Mean monthly bill trajectory across baseline and endline survey waves

Each line traces the mean monthly electricity bill (PKR, most recent billing cycle as recorded from the physical bill at interview) for one randomization arm between baseline (December 2022) and endline (2024). Four observations with monthly bills exceeding PKR 100,000 are excluded. The control group shows a slight increase of PKR 315 over the period. All four treatment arms show larger increases, consistent with a positive treatment effect on billed consumption, though all DiD confidence intervals span zero given the wide within-arm variance.

Midline (September 2023) did not collect electricity bill amounts from respondents; the chart connects baseline directly to endline for each arm. Four observations with M2_1 > PKR 100,000 excluded from means (3 in Control across waves, 1 in T2 at endline).

Kernel density estimate of monthly electricity bill amounts by group and wave

Smoothed density curves approximate the distribution of monthly electricity bills (PKR) for the control group at baseline (navy solid), the pooled treatment group at baseline (gold solid), and the pooled treatment group at endline (gold dashed). At baseline, both distributions share a similar left-skewed profile with a modal bill below PKR 5,000. By endline, the treatment group distribution shifts rightward relative to baseline, reflecting an increase in average billed amounts among treated households over the study period. The control group distribution does not shift in the same direction.

Density approximated from 18 histogram bins (bin width PKR 2,500, range PKR 0 to 45,000), smoothed using line tension 0.4. Control endline omitted for clarity. n: control baseline 313, treated baseline 1,252, treated endline 1,144.

Enumerator-observed illicit connection rates by arm and survey wave

Bars show the percentage of households where an enumerator observed a hooked (illicit) electrical connection at the time of interview, by randomization arm and survey wave. Observed theft rates are a noisy proxy for actual connection behavior and are subject to enumerator visibility constraints. The control group declined from 19.0 percent at baseline to 17.2 percent at endline. Across treatment arms, responses were heterogeneous: T4 (highest subsidy tier, mean PKR 1,183 per month) and T2 showed net decreases relative to baseline, while T1 and T3 showed net increases. No arm exhibits a statistically clear treatment effect on this measure given the sample sizes and wide confidence intervals.

P1 variable: enumerator observation of hooked connection (1 = Yes, 2 = No). Unsure and do-not-know responses excluded. Control: 288-303 obs; treatment arms: 274-298 obs per wave.

Subsidy disbursement scale and payment confirmation at midline

The navy bars show the mean monthly subsidy amount (PKR) received per household at midline by treatment tier, confirming the graduated subsidy structure across T1 to T4. The gold line and right axis show the percentage of treatment households in each arm who confirmed receipt of their January 2023 payment at the midline interview. Confirmation rates ranged from 93.8 percent (T2) to 97.5 percent (T4). Across all treatment arms, DFCA disbursed a total of PKR 805,570 to 1,116 households and collected photographic receipt evidence from 1,060 of those households.

xx_feb_pay: calculated subsidy entitlement for February 2023 billing cycle. payment variable: 1 = household confirmed receipt of January payment. Source: Midline survey, September 2023.

Field observations

What we observed.

Treatment households maintained higher billed consumption relative to the control group trend by endline.

After excluding four implausible bill amounts exceeding PKR 100,000, the control group mean monthly bill increased by PKR 315 between baseline and endline. All four treatment arms showed larger increases relative to control, producing DiD estimates ranging from PKR 484 (T4) to PKR 1,184 (T1). All 95 percent confidence intervals span zero. These are simple means-based DiD estimates without feeder-level clustering adjustments and indicate a directional pattern only.

Source: Electricity billing module (M2_1), baseline and endline. Charsadda District, 2022 and 2024.

DFCA disbursed PKR 805,570 across 1,116 treatment households, verified at a 95.0 percent confirmation rate.

Monthly subsidy amounts averaged PKR 391 per household in T1, PKR 295 in T2, PKR 687 in T3, and PKR 1,183 in T4, reflecting the graduated tier structure. At the midline survey, enumerators cross-checked each payment against the household's official electricity bill and collected photographic receipts from 1,060 households. Payment confirmation rates across all arms ranged from 93.8 to 97.5 percent.

Source: Midline payment module (xx_feb_pay, payment variable). September 2023.

Observed illicit connections showed a heterogeneous pattern across treatment arms, with no arm exhibiting a clear directional effect.

At baseline, hooked connection rates ranged from 14.2 percent (T3) to 21.1 percent (T4) across arms. By endline, T4 showed the largest net reduction (-4.9 percentage points) while T3 increased (+8.9 percentage points). The DiD for T4 relative to control was -2.3 percentage points, and for T3 was +11.4 percentage points. All DiD estimates for theft carry confidence intervals that span zero, making directional inference inconclusive from these survey-based observation data.

Source: P1 enumerator observation, baseline and endline. Charsadda District, 2022 and 2024.

Baseline household profiles were similar across arms, supporting the validity of the randomization.

At baseline, mean household head age was 42.9 years and 99.5 percent of household heads were male. Mean household size was 6.7 persons. Of household heads with education data (n=1,648), 49.0 percent reported no formal education, and 12.0 percent held Intermediate level qualifications or above (Inter, Bachelors, or Masters). After excluding two implausible bill observations in the control group, mean monthly bill amounts at baseline ranged from PKR 10,238 (T1) to PKR 11,296 (T2), with substantial within-arm variance consistent with a heterogeneous sample across three tehsils.

Source: Baseline household roster and electricity billing module. Charsadda District, December 2022.

DFCA mobilized electricity subsidy payments to users in Charsadda District over a six-month intervention window beginning January 2023. Eligible households were enrolled through a baseline census conducted in December 2022 across three tehsils: Charsadda, Tangi, and Shabqadar. Randomization allocated 1,366 households across four subsidy treatment tiers, with 332 households assigned to a no-intervention control group. DFCA field teams coordinated payment delivery via mobile money platforms, collecting mobile account details at baseline and verifying receipt at midline through structured interview questions and photographic evidence. Managing this financial intervention required establishing substantial community trust across a geographically dispersed sample in rural Charsadda. DFCA executed the disbursements with strict operational integrity: enumerators cross-checked each payment against official PESCO bill reference numbers, documented receipt photographs for 1,060 of the 1,116 enrolled households, and flagged discrepancies for follow-up during the midline survey round. The midline survey conducted in September 2023 confirmed that 95.0 percent of payment recipients had received their January 2023 subsidy at the correct calculated amount.

"Across 1,116 enrolled treatment households, DFCA field teams confirmed receipt of PKR 805,570 in electricity subsidies at a 95.0 percent confirmation rate, supported by photographic bill receipts collected at midline."
